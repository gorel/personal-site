Quiz

Question 1: What will be printed after running the following program?

total = 0
  for num in range(5):
    total += num
  print(total)

a. 10
b. 15
c. 01234
d. 012345

Question 2: What will be printed after running the following program?

result = 0
  my_list = [1, 3, 5, 2, 7, 1, 1, 1]
  index = 0
  while my_list[index] != 7:
    result += my_list[index]

  print(result)

a. 11
b. 18
c. 21
d. (error)

  • [guessing_game] Write a program called guessing_game.py that lets a user guess a random number. First, your program should ask the user what the maximum bounds should be. Next, tell the user you've thought of a number and ask for their first guess. Tell the user whether the number you're thinking of is higher or lower. Continue until the user correctly guesses the number, then tell them how many guesses it took. In case this is unclear, imagine the following game below:
$ python3 guessing_game.py
  Welcome to the guessing game!
  What should the maximum number be? 100
  Okay, I've thought of a number between 1 and 100!  (Imagine the computer randomly got 56 here)
  What's your guess? 50
  Nope! My number is higher.
  What's your guess? 70
  Nope! My number is lower.
  What's your guess? 60
  Nope! My number is lower.
  What's your guess? 55
  Nope! My number is higher.
  What's your guess? 56
  You got it!
  You took 5 guesses to find my number. Not bad!
